Sources

Bibliography
for the Congo Free State.

The Free State scholarship is dominated, in English-language popular reception, by Adam Hochschild's 1998 King Leopold's Ghost. The principal Belgian-language scholarship — particularly the multi-volume work of Jules Marchal — is the documentary foundation. The contemporary reform-campaign documents are extensively preserved in the British Foreign Office records at Kew and in the Anti-Slavery International archive in London.

A reader's reference.

General works

The Free State period

  • Hochschild, Adam. King Leopold's Ghost: A Story of Greed, Terror, and Heroism in Colonial Africa. Boston: Houghton Mifflin, 1998. The standard popular English-language account; widely read.
  • Marchal, Jules. E.D. Morel contre Léopold II: L'histoire du Congo, 1900–1910. 2 vols. Paris: L'Harmattan, 1996. French-language; the documentary foundation, drawing on the Belgian state archives.
  • Marchal, Jules. Lord Leverhulme's Ghosts: Colonial Exploitation in the Congo. Trans. Martin Thom. London: Verso, 2008.
  • Stengers, Jean. Congo: Mythes et réalités. Brussels: Éditions Racine, 2007. The standard Belgian scholarly synthesis.

Chapter I–II — Leopold and the Berlin Conference

The strategic preparation, 1865–1885

  • Emerson, Barbara. Leopold II of the Belgians: King of Colonialism. London: Weidenfeld & Nicolson, 1979.
  • Stengers, Jean, and Jan Vansina. "King Leopold's Congo, 1886–1908." In The Cambridge History of Africa, Volume 6, ed. Roland Oliver and G.N. Sanderson, 315–358. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1985.
  • Pakenham, Thomas. The Scramble for Africa, 1876–1912. London: Weidenfeld & Nicolson, 1991.
  • Förster, Stig, Wolfgang J. Mommsen, and Ronald Robinson, eds. Bismarck, Europe, and Africa: The Berlin Africa Conference 1884–1885 and the Onset of Partition. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1988.

Chapter III — The conquest

Stanley and the Force Publique

  • Jeal, Tim. Stanley: The Impossible Life of Africa's Greatest Explorer. London: Faber & Faber, 2007.
  • Newman, James L. Imperial Footprints: Henry Morton Stanley's African Journeys. Washington, DC: Brassey's, 2004.
  • Stanley, Henry M. The Congo and the Founding of Its Free State. 2 vols. London: Sampson Low, 1885. Stanley's own contemporary account of the operations.
  • Northrup, David. Beyond the Bend in the River: African Labor in Eastern Zaire, 1865–1940. Athens, OH: Ohio University Center for International Studies, 1988.

Chapter IV — The rubber system

The quota regime, 1890–1908

  • Harms, Robert. "The End of Red Rubber: A Reassessment." Journal of African History 16.1 (1975): 73–88.
  • Vansina, Jan. Being Colonized: The Kuba Experience in Rural Congo, 1880–1960. Madison: University of Wisconsin Press, 2010.
  • Hunt, Nancy Rose. A Nervous State: Violence, Remedies, and Reverie in Colonial Congo. Durham: Duke University Press, 2016.
  • Vangroenweghe, Daniel. Du sang sur les lianes: Léopold II et son Congo. Brussels: Didier Hatier, 1986. French-language; the principal contemporary Belgian critical study.

Chapter V — The reform campaign

1899–1908

  • Morel, E.D. King Leopold's Rule in Africa. London: William Heinemann, 1904. The principal contemporary reform-campaign book.
  • Morel, E.D. Red Rubber: The Story of the Rubber Slave Trade Flourishing on the Congo in the Year of Grace 1906. London: T. Fisher Unwin, 1906.
  • Casement, Roger. The Casement Report. London: HMSO, 1904. The principal contemporary diplomatic document, originally Cmd. 1933.
  • Twain, Mark. King Leopold's Soliloquy: A Defense of His Congo Rule. Boston: P.R. Warren Co., 1905.
  • Cline, Catherine Ann. E.D. Morel, 1873–1924: The Strategies of Protest. Belfast: Blackstaff, 1980.
  • Inglis, Brian. Roger Casement. London: Hodder & Stoughton, 1973.

Chapter VI — Annexation

1908

  • Slade, Ruth. King Leopold's Congo: Aspects of the Development of Race Relations in the Congo Independent State. London: Oxford University Press, 1962.
  • Cattier, Félicien. Étude sur la situation de l'État indépendant du Congo. Brussels: Larcier, 1906.

Chapter VII — The toll

Demographic accounting

  • Vansina, Jan. "Population Movements and the Emergence of New Socio-Political Forms in Africa." In UNESCO General History of Africa, Volume V, ed. Bethwell Allan Ogot, 46–73. Berkeley: University of California Press, 1992.
  • Roes, Aldwin. "Towards a History of Mass Violence in the État Indépendant du Congo, 1885–1908." South African Historical Journal 62.4 (2010): 634–670. A careful contemporary methodological assessment of the demographic estimates.

Chapter VIII — After the Free State

The Belgian Congo and Congolese independence

  • Hochschild, Adam. King Leopold's Ghost, see above; substantial closing chapters on the post-1908 period.
  • De Witte, Ludo. The Assassination of Lumumba. Trans. Ann Wright and Renée Fenby. London: Verso, 2001.
  • Van Reybrouck, David. Congo: The Epic History of a People. Trans. Sam Garrett. New York: HarperCollins, 2014. The current standard popular history of the entire Congolese experience.
  • Stanard, Matthew G. The Leopard, the Lion, and the Cock: Colonial Memories and Monuments in Belgium. Leuven: Leuven University Press, 2019. On the Belgian public memory.

Primary archives

The documentary record

  • Anti-Slavery International Archive, London. Holds the Alice Seeley Harris photographic collection and the Congo Reform Association papers.
  • The Roger Casement papers, UK National Archives at Kew (PRO classmark CO 879 and FO 10).
  • The Royal Museum for Central Africa (AfricaMuseum), Tervuren, Belgium. Substantial Free State-period artefacts and partial archives. Reopened in renovated form in 2018.
  • The Scheut and Missionary archives in Belgium, holding substantial first-hand missionary accounts from the period.
