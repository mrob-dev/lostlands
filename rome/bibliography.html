Sources

Bibliography
for the Roman Empire.

The Roman historiography is the largest of any pre-modern state. The English-language standard reference is the multi-volume Cambridge Ancient History (now in its second edition). For accessible single-volume treatments: Mary Beard for the empire's beginnings, Peter Heather and Bryan Ward-Perkins for the western fall, Judith Herrin and Anthony Kaldellis for the eastern continuation.

A reader's reference.

General works

The whole empire

  • Beard, Mary. SPQR: A History of Ancient Rome. London: Profile, 2015.
  • Goldsworthy, Adrian. Pax Romana: War, Peace and Conquest in the Roman World. London: Weidenfeld & Nicolson, 2016.
  • The Cambridge Ancient History. 14 vols, 2nd ed. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1970–2005. The scholarly standard reference.
  • Gibbon, Edward. The History of the Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire. 6 vols, 1776–1789. Modern edition: ed. David Womersley, London: Penguin, 1994. The eighteenth-century classic; still readable, still widely cited.
