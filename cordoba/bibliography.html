Bibliography
for the Caliphate of Córdoba.

The al-Andalus scholarship has been one of the most-contested historical fields of the past quarter-century. Hugh Kennedy is the standard scholarly authority on the political history; María Rosa Menocal popularised the convivencia thesis; Darío Fernández-Morera and David Nirenberg have offered substantial critiques. The principal medieval Arabic sources are Ibn Khaldun, Ibn al-Khatib, and Ibn al-Idhari.

A reader's reference.

General works

Al-Andalus, 711–1492

  • Kennedy, Hugh. Muslim Spain and Portugal: A Political History of al-Andalus. London: Longman, 1996. The standard English-language political history.
  • Catlos, Brian A. Kingdoms of Faith: A New History of Islamic Spain. London: Hurst, 2018.
  • Fletcher, Richard. Moorish Spain. 2nd ed. Berkeley: University of California Press, 2006.
  • Collins, Roger. Caliphs and Kings: Spain, 796–1031. Oxford: Wiley-Blackwell, 2012.

Chapters I–II — The Umayyad refuge and the caliphal proclamation

756–929

  • Safran, Janina M. The Second Umayyad Caliphate: The Articulation of Caliphal Legitimacy in al-Andalus. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 2000.
  • Manzano Moreno, Eduardo. Conquistadores, emires y califas: Los omeyas y la formación de al-Andalus. Barcelona: Crítica, 2006. Spanish-language; the standard contemporary monograph.
  • Brann, Ross. Power in the Portrayal: Representations of Jews and Muslims in Eleventh- and Twelfth-Century Islamic Spain. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2002.

The palace city

Chapter IV — Al-Hakam II and the library

The high cultural moment

  • Wasserstein, David J. The Caliphate in the West: An Islamic Political Institution in the Iberian Peninsula. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1993.
  • Burnett, Charles. The Introduction of Arabic Learning into England. London: British Library, 1997.
  • Ribera y Tarragó, Julián. Música árabe medieval y su influencia en la española. Madrid: Voluntad, 1929. The classic study of the Cordoban musical heritage.

  • Scales, Peter C. The Fall of the Caliphate of Córdoba: Berbers and Andalusis in Conflict. Leiden: Brill, 1994.

1031–1492

  • Wasserstein, David J. The Rise and Fall of the Party-Kings: Politics and Society in Islamic Spain, 1002–1086. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 1985.

Chapter IX — After Córdoba and the convivencia debate

1492 to today

  • Menocal, María Rosa. The Ornament of the World: How Muslims, Jews, and Christians Created a Culture of Tolerance in Medieval Spain. Boston: Little, Brown, 2002. The popular convivencia thesis statement.
  • Fernández-Morera, Darío. The Myth of the Andalusian Paradise: Muslims, Christians, and Jews under Islamic Rule in Medieval Spain. Wilmington, DE: ISI Books, 2016. The principal critical revisionist statement.
  • Nirenberg, David. Communities of Violence: Persecution of Minorities in the Middle Ages. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 1996.
  • Soyer, François. The Persecution of the Jews and Muslims of Portugal: King Manuel I and the End of Religious Tolerance (1496–7). Leiden: Brill, 2007.
  • Hess, Andrew C. The Forgotten Frontier: A History of the Sixteenth-Century Ibero-African Frontier. Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 1978.

Primary sources

Medieval Andalusi voices

  • Ibn Khaldun. The Muqaddimah: An Introduction to History. Trans. Franz Rosenthal. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 1958.
  • Ibn Hazm. The Ring of the Dove: A Treatise on the Art and Practice of Arab Love. Trans. A.J. Arberry. London: Luzac, 1953. The classic Cordoban work on love and society, c. 1022.
  • Ibn al-Khatib. Al-Ihata fi akhbar Gharnata. 14th-century Granada-period chronicle; selections in trans. by various scholars.
  • Maimonides (Moses ben Maimon). The Guide of the Perplexed. Trans. Shlomo Pines. Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 1963.
