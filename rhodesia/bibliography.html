Bibliography  ·  Rhodesia

What we read,
how we know.

A short bibliography of the works on which this volume relies, with notes for the curious reader.

General histories

Standard surveys

  • Robert Blake, A History of Rhodesia, Eyre Methuen, 1977. The standard one-volume history written during the UDI period; politically conservative in framing but the most thorough single account.
  • Martin Meredith, Mugabe: Power, Plunder, and the Struggle for Zimbabwe, Public Affairs, 2002. A substantial post-1980 narrative; chapter 1 covers the Rhodesian background.
  • Ian Phimister, An Economic and Social History of Zimbabwe 1890–1948, Longman, 1988. The standard treatment of the BSAC and early self-government periods.

UDI and sanctions

The 1965 declaration and after

  • Donal Lowry, The Crown, the Empire and the Royal Family in Rhodesia 1923–80, in Round Table, 99:407, 2010. On the constitutional anomaly of Rhodesia's relationship with the British Crown.
  • Carl Watts, Rhodesia's Unilateral Declaration of Independence: An International History, Palgrave Macmillan, 2012. The most thorough recent treatment of the diplomatic process around UDI.
  • Thomas Bingham and S.M. Gray, Report on the Supply of Petroleum and Petroleum Products to Rhodesia, HMSO, 1978. The British government's investigation of sanctions-busting in the petroleum trade.

The Bush War

1972–1979

  • Paul Moorcraft and Peter McLaughlin, The Rhodesian War: A Military History, Pen and Sword Military, 2008. The standard military history; technically detailed and politically restrained.
  • J.R.T. Wood, So Far and No Further!: Rhodesia's Bid for Independence During the Retreat from Empire, Trafford, 2005. A pro-Rhodesian account from a senior military officer, valuable for the operational detail.
  • Norma Kriger, Zimbabwe's Guerrilla War: Peasant Voices, Cambridge University Press, 1992. A field-research-based account of the war from the African rural perspective.
  • Ken Flower, Serving Secretly: An Intelligence Chief on Record. Rhodesia into Zimbabwe 1964–1981, Galago, 1987. Memoir of the head of the Rhodesian Central Intelligence Organisation; particularly important for the diplomatic context.

Lancaster House and after

The settlement and the transition

  • Robin Renwick, Unconventional Diplomacy in Southern Africa, Macmillan, 1997. By the British ambassador who handled the immediate post-Lancaster transition; a careful first-hand account.
  • Stephen Chan, Robert Mugabe: A Life of Power and Violence, I.B. Tauris, 2003. A short and analytically careful biography of the leader who emerged from Lancaster House.

Memoirs and primary sources

First-hand accounts

  • Ian Smith, The Great Betrayal: The Memoirs of Ian Douglas Smith, John Blake, 1997. Smith's own account of UDI and after — unrepentant, vivid, and a primary source for understanding the white Rhodesian political imagination.
  • Joshua Nkomo, The Story of My Life, Methuen, 1984. Nkomo's autobiography, ending shortly after independence; the standard account of ZAPU's politics.
  • National Archives of Zimbabwe, Harare. The main archive of Rhodesian-era government records, now under Zimbabwean administration. Access policies have varied substantially since 1980; researchers should contact the archive in advance.

Older country

Great Zimbabwe and pre-colonial history

  • Innocent Pikirayi, The Zimbabwe Culture: Origins and Decline of Southern Zambezian States, AltaMira, 2001. The standard archaeological treatment of Great Zimbabwe and the successor Mutapa state.
  • Peter Garlake, Great Zimbabwe, Thames and Hudson, 1973. The book whose publication in Rhodesia in 1973 led to the Rhodesian Department of Information ordering Garlake's deportation; a foundational text in African archaeology.
