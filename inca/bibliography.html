Bibliography
for the Inca Empire.

The Inca scholarship combines Spanish-colonial chronicle sources (Cieza de León, Sarmiento, Guaman Poma) with modern archaeology and a substantial body of contemporary work on the khipus. Terence D'Altroy's The Incas is the standard one-volume reference; John Hemming's older Conquest of the Incas remains canonical for the conquest period.

A reader's reference.

General works

Tahuantinsuyu

  • D'Altroy, Terence N. The Incas. 2nd ed. Oxford: Wiley-Blackwell, 2014. The standard one-volume contemporary reference.
  • McEwan, Gordon F. The Incas: New Perspectives. New York: W.W. Norton, 2006.
  • Rostworowski de Diez Canseco, María. History of the Inca Realm. Trans. Harry B. Iceland. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1999.
  • Mann, Charles C. 1491: New Revelations of the Americas Before Columbus. New York: Knopf, 2005. The accessible single-volume synthesis of pre-Columbian American history.
