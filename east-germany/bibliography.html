Sources

Bibliography
for East Germany.

The English-language GDR scholarship has been one of the active fields in post-1989 European history, with Mary Fulbrook and Charles Maier the standard reference authors. The Stasi archive (BStU, now part of the Federal Archives) was opened to substantive research in 1992 and remains the largest accessible state-security archive of any twentieth-century communist regime.

A reader's reference.

General works

The whole forty-one years

  • Fulbrook, Mary. The People's State: East German Society from Hitler to Honecker. New Haven: Yale University Press, 2005. The standard one-volume social history.
  • Fulbrook, Mary. Anatomy of a Dictatorship: Inside the GDR, 1949–1989. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1995.
  • Maier, Charles S. Dissolution: The Crisis of Communism and the End of East Germany. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 1997. The standard treatment of the collapse.
  • Major, Patrick. Behind the Berlin Wall: East Germany and the Frontiers of Power. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2010.

Chapters I–III — Soviet zone, Ulbricht, the Wall

Founding and consolidation, 1945–1961

  • Naimark, Norman M. The Russians in Germany: A History of the Soviet Zone of Occupation, 1945–1949. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1995.
  • Pritchard, Gareth. The Making of the GDR, 1945–53. Manchester: Manchester University Press, 2000.
  • Harrison, Hope M. Driving the Soviets Up the Wall: Soviet–East German Relations, 1953–1961. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2003.
  • Kempe, Frederick. Berlin 1961. New York: Putnam, 2011.

Chapters IV–V — Honecker, everyday life

The long stability, 1971–1989

  • Betts, Paul. Within Walls: Private Life in the German Democratic Republic. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2010.
  • Pence, Katherine, and Paul Betts, eds. Socialist Modern: East German Everyday Culture and Politics. Ann Arbor: University of Michigan Press, 2008.
  • Funder, Anna. Stasiland: Stories from Behind the Berlin Wall. London: Granta, 2003. Journalistic but vivid; widely read.
  • Garton Ash, Timothy. The File: A Personal History. London: HarperCollins, 1997.
  • Wolle, Stefan. Die heile Welt der Diktatur: Alltag und Herrschaft in der DDR 1971–1989. Berlin: Christoph Links, 1998. German-language; the principal account of late-GDR everyday life.

Chapter VI — The Stasi

The state security apparatus

  • Bruce, Gary. The Firm: The Inside Story of the Stasi. New York: Oxford University Press, 2010.
  • Childs, David, and Richard Popplewell. The Stasi: The East German Intelligence and Security Service. Basingstoke: Macmillan, 1996.
  • Gieseke, Jens. The History of the Stasi: East Germany's Secret Police, 1945–1990. New York: Berghahn, 2014.
  • The BStU/Bundesarchiv Stasi Records Archive at Berlin holds about 111 km of files; substantial portions are accessible to applicants for personal-file inquiries and to researchers under the 1991 Stasi Records Act.

Chapters VII–VIII — Revolution and afterwards

1989, reunification, and the long aftermath

  • Sarotte, Mary Elise. The Collapse: The Accidental Opening of the Berlin Wall. New York: Basic Books, 2014.
  • Sarotte, Mary Elise. 1989: The Struggle to Create Post-Cold War Europe. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2009.
  • Zelikow, Philip, and Condoleezza Rice. Germany Unified and Europe Transformed: A Study in Statecraft. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1995.
  • Hertle, Hans-Hermann. The Berlin Wall Story: Biography of a Monument. Berlin: Ch. Links, 2011.
  • Engler, Wolfgang. Die Ostdeutschen: Kunde von einem verlorenen Land. Berlin: Aufbau, 1999. German-language; an Ossi-perspective social analysis of the post-reunification period.

Primary sources, fiction, films

The GDR's own voice

  • Wolf, Christa. Divided Heaven (1963), Patterns of Childhood (1976), Cassandra (1983). Various English translations.
  • Brussig, Thomas. Helden wie wir (1995). English: Heroes Like Us, trans. John Brownjohn, 1997.
  • Henckel von Donnersmarck, Florian, dir. The Lives of Others (Das Leben der Anderen). Germany, 2006. The principal English-accessible feature-film treatment of the Stasi.
  • Wenders, Wim, dir. Der Himmel über Berlin (Wings of Desire). Germany, 1987.
