Sources

Bibliography
for Green Ukraine.

The bibliography on the Far Eastern Ukrainian community is small and substantially Ukrainian-language. The principal English-language work is Ivan Svit's 1972 study. The post-1991 Ukrainian academic literature has expanded the field, though it is not extensively translated. The Far Eastern Soviet archives in Vladivostok and Khabarovsk hold the principal documentary material from the period.

A reader's reference.

General works on the Ukrainian Far Eastern community

The Far Eastern diaspora

  • Svit, Ivan. Ukrainian-Japanese Relations, 1903–1945: A Historical Survey. New York: Ukrainian Historical Association, 1972. The principal early English-language treatment, by a member of the émigré community.
  • Chornomaz, Vyacheslav. "Green Ukraine: The Ukrainian Far Eastern Republic." In Encyclopedia of Ukraine, vol. 2, ed. Volodymyr Kubijovyč. Toronto: University of Toronto Press, 1988.
  • Černiavs'kyj, Vasyl. Zelena Ukrayina. Lviv: Ukrayins'ki Visti, 1929. Ukrainian-language; the principal contemporary émigré reference.

Chapter I — The Stolypin settlement

Ukrainian agricultural migration to the Russian Far East

  • Treadgold, Donald W. The Great Siberian Migration: Government and Peasant in Resettlement from Emancipation to the First World War. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 1957.
  • Coquin, François-Xavier. La Sibérie: Peuplement et immigration paysanne au XIXe siècle. Paris: Institut d'études slaves, 1969. French-language; the standard study of nineteenth-century Russian peasant migration east.
  • Forsyth, James. A History of the Peoples of Siberia: Russia's North Asian Colony 1581–1990. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1992.

Chapters II–III — The 1917 organisation and Congresses

1917–1918

  • Hrushevsky, Mykhailo. History of Ukraine-Rus'. Vol. 9–10. Toronto: Canadian Institute of Ukrainian Studies Press, 2010. The classic Ukrainian national-history multivolume; covers the 1917 period.
  • Subtelny, Orest. Ukraine: A History. 4th ed. Toronto: University of Toronto Press, 2009.
  • Velychenko, Stephen. Painting Imperialism and Nationalism Red: The Ukrainian Marxist Critique of Russian Communist Rule in Ukraine, 1918–1925. Toronto: University of Toronto Press, 2015.

Chapter IV — The Civil War

The Far Eastern Russian Civil War, 1918–1922

  • Stephan, John J. The Russian Far East: A History. Stanford: Stanford University Press, 1994. The standard one-volume English-language history of the Russian Far East.
  • Pereira, N.G.O. White Siberia: The Politics of Civil War. Montreal: McGill-Queen's University Press, 1996.
  • Smele, Jonathan D. Civil War in Siberia: The Anti-Bolshevik Government of Admiral Kolchak, 1918–1920. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1996.
  • White, John Albert. The Siberian Intervention. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 1950. On the Allied intervention forces.

Chapter V — The Soviet absorption

1922–1939

  • Martin, Terry. The Affirmative Action Empire: Nations and Nationalism in the Soviet Union, 1923–1939. Ithaca: Cornell University Press, 2001. The standard work on Soviet korenizatsiia policy.
  • Hirsch, Francine. Empire of Nations: Ethnographic Knowledge and the Making of the Soviet Union. Ithaca: Cornell University Press, 2005.
  • Stephan, John J. The Russian Far East, see above; chapters 6–9 cover the early Soviet Far East.

Chapter VI — After Green Ukraine

The contemporary community

  • Wilson, Andrew. The Ukrainians: Unexpected Nation. 4th ed. New Haven: Yale University Press, 2015.
  • Plokhy, Serhii. The Gates of Europe: A History of Ukraine. 2nd ed. New York: Basic Books, 2021.
  • Marples, David R. The Ukrainians: An Unexpected Nation. London: Routledge, 2002.
  • Chornomaz, Vyacheslav. Zelenyi Klyn (Ukrains'kyi Dalekyi Skhid). Vladivostok: DalRPRoman, 2011. Ukrainian-language; the principal contemporary study of the Far Eastern community.

Primary archives

The documentary record

  • The Russian State Historical Archive of the Far East (RGIA DV) at Vladivostok. Holds Tsarist and early Soviet-period administrative records.
  • The Ivan Svit papers at the Ukrainian Cultural Heritage Village Archive, Edmonton, Canada.
  • The Shevchenko Scientific Society archive, New York.
  • The Canadian Institute of Ukrainian Studies (CIUS), University of Alberta. Significant émigré papers from the Far Eastern community.
