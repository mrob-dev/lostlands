Sources

Bibliography
for the Soviet Union.

The English-language Soviet historiography is the largest of any modern state. Substantial new work has appeared since the post-1991 opening of the Soviet archives, particularly Stephen Kotkin's three-volume Stalin biography and Vladislav Zubok's account of the collapse. Robert Service remains the standard single-volume narrative.

A reader's reference.

The whole period

  • Suny, Ronald Grigor. The Soviet Experiment: Russia, the USSR, and the Successor States. 2nd ed. New York: Oxford University Press, 2010.

Primary archives

The documentary record

  • The Russian State Archive of Socio-Political History (RGASPI), Moscow, holds the Party archives (Politburo, Central Committee, Lenin/Stalin personal funds).
  • The Hoover Institution at Stanford holds substantial Soviet-era microfilm collections.
  • The Sakharov Center in Moscow (founded 1996) holds the dissident-period documentary record.
  • Memorial Society (Russia), founded 1989, was the principal NGO archive of Stalin-era repression; dissolved by Russian court order in 2021, its archives are partly preserved abroad.
