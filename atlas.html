A note on the markers

Use as
a planning tool.

The map is intended as a planning aid. Each marker corresponds to a "stop" in one of the twelve volumes' travel sections, and clicking through takes you to that volume's travel page, where the full description and practical notes live. The travel routes themselves — three or so per volume — are described in each volume's Routes page.

The geographic coverage is wider than any single volume implies: the Ottoman and Roman travel sections each have stops in eight countries; the Persia volume has stops in five (Iran, Uzbekistan, Afghanistan, India, the United States); the Soviet volume has stops in nine. The Lost Lands library is not, even by its travel layer, an Eurocentric project.

Coordinates are approximate (~5 km accuracy is sufficient at this scale). Where a stop describes a region rather than a point — the Matadi-Kinshasa railway, Hadrian's Wall, the southern Inca Qhapaq Ñan — the marker is placed at a representative midpoint.
